My Focus: The Cosmos

My main interest lies in the study of Gravitation and Cosmology. We investigate the largest structures and the deepest mysteries of the universe.

Our work involves exploring the nature of Dark Matter and analyzing how massive objects like black holes warp space-time, as described by General Relativity.

The Dual Nature of Science

Physics gives us incredible power to heal and build, but with that comes immense responsibility.

The development of the nuclear bomb by J. Robert Oppenheimer and his team during the Manhattan Project changed the world forever. It serves as a stark historical reminder of the ethical considerations in science.

We must always be cautious regarding the martial and destructive applications of our discoveries.

Research & Geometry

My research focuses on Mathematical Physics, specifically applying Differential Geometry to General Relativity.

I presented work on this topic, titled "Application of Differential Geometry to General Relativity," at an international congress in 2024.

This complex mathematical language is essential for describing how gravity bends the universe, unifying abstract math with physical reality.
